Calculation Guide & Correlations

Configuration Inputs

  • Frame Size (FPV): Prop size proxy. Larger frames increase thrust potential, weight, and speed potential.
  • Motor KV: RPM per volt. Higher KV increases speed and peak current draw, but usually reduces efficiency.
  • Battery Chemistry: LiPo supports higher discharge; Li-Ion has better energy density but lower safe C-rate.
  • Battery Capacity: Higher mAh increases energy and flight time, but also adds battery weight.
  • Flight Controller: Affects electronics weight contribution.
  • Camera: Changes payload weight and impacts total mass.
  • VTX Power: Improves link range, increases power-system load and small weight overhead.
  • Wingspan (Fixed Wing): Larger wings improve cruise efficiency but generally reduce top speed.
  • Wing Type (Fixed Wing): Modifies drag/efficiency assumptions and therefore speed, current, and range behavior.
  • Propeller Selection (APC): Auto/manual prop choice impacts thrust and power modeling when APC data is available.
  • Analyze Impact By: Controls which parameter is swept in comparison charts.

Calculated Outputs

  • Total Weight: Sum of frame/airframe, motor(s), battery, electronics, camera, VTX, and wiring assumptions.
  • Estimated Flight Time: Derived from usable battery energy and average current demand.
  • Payload Capacity: Estimated max safe takeoff mass minus current configuration mass.
  • Max Speed: Function of KV, voltage (cell count), geometry (frame or wing), and chemistry power factor.
  • Power-to-Weight: Estimated available power normalized by mass.
  • Range (Line of Sight): Minimum of signal-limited range and time/speed-limited range.
  • Battery Discharge Rate: Estimated burst current divided by battery Ah, shown as required C-rating.
  • Current Draw (Hover/Cruise): FPV hover current or fixed-wing cruise current estimate.
  • Component Impact Analysis: Highlights heaviest component, limiting factor, and best next improvement.
